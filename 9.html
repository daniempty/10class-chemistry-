Chapter 9: Chemical Equilibrium - Short Answers

1. Define law of mass action.

The law of mass action states that the rate of a chemical reaction is directly proportional to the product of the active masses of the reactants, each raised to the power equal to its stoichiometric coefficient in the balanced chemical equation.

2. Differentiate between reactants and products.

Reactants: Substances that take part in a chemical reaction.
Products: Substances formed as a result of a chemical reaction.
Example: In H₂ + Cl₂ → 2HCl, H₂ and Cl₂ are reactants, and HCl is the product.

3. What are irreversible reactions? Give an example.

An irreversible reaction proceeds only in one direction from reactants to products and cannot reverse under normal conditions.
Example: Combustion of methane: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

4. Write characteristics of irreversible reactions.

- Proceed only in one direction.
- Products are stable and do not revert to reactants.
Example: Neutralization reaction: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

5. Define equilibrium constant.

The equilibrium constant (Kc) is the ratio of concentrations of products to reactants at equilibrium, each raised to the power of their respective stoichiometric coefficients.

6. Define the extent of a reaction.

The extent of a reaction measures how far a reaction proceeds toward completion by comparing equilibrium concentrations of reactants and products.

7. Define dynamic equilibrium with an example.

Dynamic equilibrium occurs when forward and reverse reactions take place at the same rate, maintaining constant concentrations.
Example: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃

8. What happens when Qc > Kc and Qc = Kc?

- If Qc > Kc, the reaction proceeds in the reverse direction.
- If Qc = Kc, the reaction is in equilibrium.

9. Write Kc expression for CO(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ CH₄(g) + H₂O(g).

Kc = [CH₄][H₂O] / [CO][H₂]³

10. How is dynamic equilibrium established?

It is established when the forward and reverse reaction rates become equal, resulting in constant concentrations.

11. Characteristics of reversible reactions.

- They occur in both forward and reverse directions.
- Never reach full completion; attain equilibrium.

12. What is static equilibrium with an example?

Static equilibrium involves no ongoing reaction or change.
Example: A book resting on a table.

13. Define reversible reactions with an example.

Reversible reactions proceed both forward and backward.
Example: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃

14. Macroscopic properties of reversible reactions.

- Concentrations remain constant.
- Physical properties do not change at equilibrium.

15. Define chemical equilibrium state.

It occurs when forward and reverse reaction rates are equal, and reactant-product concentrations are constant.

16. Write Kc for 2NO₂ ⇌ N₂O₄.

Kc = [N₂O₄] / [NO₂]²

17. Significance of large Kc value.

Indicates product formation is favored.

18. Calculate equilibrium units for N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃.

Units: 1 / (mol/dm³)²

19. Write Kc for H₂ + I₂ ⇌ 2HI.

Kc = [HI]² / ([H₂][I₂])

20. Define forward and reverse reactions.

- Forward: Reactants produce products.
- Reverse: Products revert to reactants.
Example: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃

21. Define homogeneous equilibrium.

An equilibrium where all substances are in the same phase.

22. Define heterogeneous equilibrium.

Equilibrium involving different phases of reactants and products.

23. Write an example of a homogeneous equilibrium reaction.

N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (gases)

24. Write an example of a heterogeneous equilibrium reaction.

CaCO₃ (s) ⇌ CaO (s) + CO₂ (g)

25. State Le Chatelier's principle.

When a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to oppose the disturbance and restore equilibrium.

26. Effect of pressure increase on N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃.

The equilibrium shifts to the side with fewer gas molecules, favoring NH₃ production.

27. Effect of temperature decrease on exothermic reactions.

Equilibrium shifts in the forward direction to produce more heat.

28. Example of a reaction involving temperature change shift.

N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃; exothermic forward reaction.

29. Define catalyst effect on equilibrium.

Catalysts speed up both forward and reverse reactions without affecting the position of equilibrium.

30. Write an industrial application of equilibrium reactions.

Haber process for NH₃ production: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃

31. What is Kp?

Kp is the equilibrium constant expressed in terms of partial pressures.
